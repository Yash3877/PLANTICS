ThePeepalTree

Scientific Name : Ficus religiosa L.
Family : Moraceae
Common Name :: Pimpal(प िंपळ)
Ficus religiosa L. is a species of fig native to the Indian subcontinent and Indochina. It is also the state tree of the Indian states of Odisha, Bihar and Haryana. It is a dry season - deciduous or semi – evergreen tree up to 30 metres (98Ft) tall and with a trunk diameter of upto 3 metres(9.8 ft).The leaves are cordate in shape with adistinctive extended drip tip.The fruits are small figs. Ficus religiosa has a lifespan ranging between 900 and 1,500 years.
Ecological Value:It provides the maximum oxygen. They aid in dispersal of seeds and can be a source of natural reforestation. The Indian flying foxes (bats) often visit Peepal tree as this tree is source of abundant food. Bats are considered ‘keystone species’ in the forest ecosystem.
Cultural Value: In Indian culture the Peepal tree is considered sacred tree and is also described by Lord Krishna in Bhagavad Gita.It is also known as the Bodhi tree or Aswatha tree.Lord Buddha achieved enlightenment under the Bodhi Vriksha. In India,the medal for the highest civilian award, Bharat Ratna,is modelled on the leaf of a Peepal tree.
Economic Importance:Ficus religiosa is used in traditional medicine for about fifty types of disorders.
