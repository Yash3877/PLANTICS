Ficusbenghalensis

Scientific Name : Ficus benghalensis L.
Family : Moraceae
Common Name :: Wad (वड)
The Banyan tree is India’s national tree, is a tree native to the Indian subcontinent. The Banyan ,which belongs to the fig family, develops accessory trunks from adventitious prop roots, allowing the tree to spread outwards indefinitely. The banyan reaches a height up to 30 metres (100 feet). The leaves of the banyan tree are large, leathery, glossy, green, and elliptical. Like other fig species, banyans bear their fruit in the form of a structure called a ‘syconium’. Flowering and fruiting takes place in the months of April to July.
Ecological Value: It’s remarkable pollination system has a fundamental impact on tropical forest ecology. Fruits of a Ficus plant attracts a host of mammals and birds, sustaining at least 1200 bird and mammal species hence known as ‘keystone species of forest ecosystem’.
Cultural Value:: In Indian culture it is known as ‘Vatvriksha’ and worshipped on Vat Pournima.
Economic Importance:Bark, wood, aerial prop roots, leaves and figs have economical and medicinal values. Ayurveda describes banyan is a cure for many ailments.
