Mahua

Scientific Name : Bassia latifolia
Family : Sapotaceae
Common Name :: Moha (मोह)
Bassia latifolia is an Indian tropical tree. It is adaptable to arid environments, being a prominent tree in tropical mixed deciduous forests. It is a fast- growing tree that grows to approximately 20 meters in height, possesses evergreen or semi-evergreen foliage.
Ecological Value:Moha though not a legume, develops mycorrhizal associations able to fix atmospheric nitrogen. Moha flowers produce a nectar that is very valuable to honey bees in periods of scarcity.
Cultural Value:Moha tree is the sacred tree of various temples in South India.
Economical Value:It is cultivated for its oleaginous seeds(producing between 20 and 200 kg of seeds annually per tree, depending on maturity).The fat is used for the care of the skin, to manufacture soap or detergents, and as a vegetable butter. It can also be used as a fuel oil. Moha trees host the tassar silkworm which produces silk traditionally used in making saris. Several parts of the tree, including the bark, are used for their medicinal properties.
